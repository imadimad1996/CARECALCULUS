# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-06-27*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** CareCalculus is a free, multilingual clinical decision support platform and medical calculator suite.
**What it does:** Provides healthcare professionals with zero-friction, highly accurate medical calculators (BMI, MAP, GCS, qSOFA, etc.) wrapped in deep clinical context, evidence-based literature, and translations (English, French, Arabic).
**Product category:** Clinical Decision Support / Medical Calculators
**Product type:** Free Web Application
**Business model:** 100% Ad-Supported (Monetized exclusively via Adsterra)

## Target Audience
**Target companies:** Hospitals, clinics, medical universities, independent practices.
**Decision-makers:** End-users (Doctors, Nurses, Medical Students, Paramedics).
**Primary use case:** Quickly calculating clinical scores at the point of care to guide immediate medical decisions (e.g., intubation thresholds, sepsis screening).
**Jobs to be done:**
- Assess patient severity rapidly and accurately.
- Avoid manual calculation errors during high-stress situations.
- Provide validated clinical thresholds and management suggestions.
**Use cases:**
- ICU nurses calculating Mean Arterial Pressure (MAP) to titrate vasopressors.
- ER doctors using the Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) to determine if a trauma patient needs intubation.

## Personas
| Persona | Cares about | Challenge | Value we promise |
|---------|-------------|-----------|------------------|
| Physician / ER Doc | Accuracy, speed, clinical context | High cognitive load during shifts, no time for complex math | Instant, validated results with evidence-based management tips |
| Nurse / Paramedic | Ease of use, mobile-friendliness | Needs rapid vitals assessment at the bedside | Zero-friction UI that works flawlessly on mobile devices |
| Medical Student | Learning, pathophysiological context | Memorizing scoring systems and their clinical significance | Deep pillar content explaining the "why" behind the scores |

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Healthcare workers need to make rapid, mathematically-driven decisions at the bedside, but manual calculations are slow and prone to human error.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- MDCalc is cluttered, English-only, and requires an app download for full features.
- EMR-integrated calculators are often clunky and take too many clicks to access.
**What it costs them:** Time, mental bandwidth, and potentially patient safety if an error occurs.
**Emotional tension:** Stress and urgency in critical care scenarios.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** MDCalc — falls short because it lacks robust multilingual support (especially Arabic) and has a more cluttered UI.
**Secondary:** EMR internal tools (Epic/Cerner) — fall short because they require logging in and navigating complex patient charts.
**Indirect:** Mental math / paper cheat sheets — fall short because they are prone to error in high-stress situations.

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Deep localization (Arabic RTL, French, English).
- Programmatic SEO architecture targeting localized queries.
- Clean, zero-friction UI tailored for rapid bedside use.
**How we do it differently:** We combine the calculator with deep "Pillar Content" (pathophysiology, intubation thresholds) so it serves as both a tool and a reference.
**Why that's better:** It builds topical authority for SEO and provides higher value to the clinician.
**Why customers choose us:** It's fast, free, multilingual, and requires no login.

**Anti-persona:** Patients trying to self-diagnose complex conditions (our tools are for clinical decision support).

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Frustration with slow, login-gated, or English-only tools.
**Pull:** The speed, mobile-responsiveness, and multilingual capabilities of CareCalculus.
**Habit:** Clinicians are very used to MDCalc.
**Anxiety:** Trusting a new platform's mathematical accuracy.

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "I need to calculate the MAP right now."
- "What's the GCS for a patient who only opens eyes to pain?"
**How they describe us:**
- "A fast medical calculator."
**Words to use:** Zero-friction, point-of-care, evidence-based, clinical decision support.
**Words to avoid:** Diagnose, treat (we provide *support*, not definitive diagnosis).
